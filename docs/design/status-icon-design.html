Rising Light — the cursor lights the last line

Your icon.png as a glyph: two hollow lines, a third line lit from the right by a run of vertical strokes, and a solid cursor (rounded rectangle) split off its end. While thinking, that light rises right→left across the last line — like a sunrise. Lines 1 & 2 stay dark; only the last line lights.

How the motion ships. A status-bar glyph is one flat colour. The sweep and blink here aren't CSS — the extension animates by swapping a few pre-rendered glyph frames on a timer (sweep ≈ 5 frames, blink = 2), which it’s free to do. Ready/Disabled are static single glyphs. icon.png is untouched.

Ready — exactly as specified

  1. Lines 1 & 2 — hollow, border only.
  2. Line 3 — hollow on the left, then vertical strokes grow denser toward the right until the end is solid (no gap) — lit by the cursor.
  3. The cursor — a fully-filled rounded rectangle, split off the end of line 3.

The states

Thinking sweeps the fill right→left on the last line only. Starting drops all lines to hollow and blinks the cursor. (Both animate live above.)

Why this direction

  • The metaphor is the mechanic. "The cursor lights the line" is the brand story and the busy indicator in one — completions literally light up as the engine works.
  • Restrained. Only the last line ever animates; the two hollow lines are a calm, constant frame.
  • Distinct without colour. Every state is a different silhouette or motion, not a recoloured copy.
